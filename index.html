MUSEUM OF CANDY

In early 2020, London will see yet another food-themed museum filled with opportunities for munching, shopping and selfie-taking. Named the Candy Museum, this 25,000-square-foot candy wonderland — located in the iconic Limelight Club space on the corner of 20th Street and Sixth Avenue — is first of three museums founded by Candy Factory. The main focus of the museum is to celebrate “Sweets, treats and fun!”

CANDY CAFÉ

Five to 10 candy makers will show you the entire candy and gummy bear-making process. Aside from viewing everything through the glass window, guests may be able to join the fun too. On average, Candy Factory makes about 10,000 gummy bears per day for all their London locations, and this café will be where they’re all made. Beyond the 50 flavors of gummy bears available for purchase, one of the highlights of the museum is a gummy bear entrance that took more than 6 months to create.

DESSERT MARKETPLACE

You’re probably visiting for the eating and the shopping. This 5,000-square-foot dessert extravaganza near the front entrance will see more than 20 vendors and confectioners from around the world making gummy bears, donuts, cookies and different pastries. What’s more, they will sell more than 500 different types of candies by the pound plus tools you may need to make your own candy at home.
